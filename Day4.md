## ðŸ›¡ Defensive Security (Blue Team)

AI is widely adopted in defensive security to:

- Speed up **threat detection and investigation**
- Correlate telemetry from logs, endpoints, and networks
- Enhance **firewalls, IDS, and SIEM systems**
- Automate responses such as:
  - Isolating compromised hosts
  - Blocking malicious emails
  - Flagging abnormal logins in real time

AI acts as an **assistant**, not a replacement, improving analyst efficiency.

## âš” Offensive Security (Red Team)

In offensive security, AI helps automate repetitive tasks such as:

- Reconnaissance and OSINT gathering
- Parsing noisy scan results
- Mapping attack surfaces

This allows penetration testers to focus on **decision-making and exploitation**
that still require human expertise.

## âš ï¸ Considerations & Risks of AI in Cyber Security

While powerful, AI is **not a silver bullet**. Key considerations include:

- AI outputs are **not always correct**
- Risk of overwhelming systems during offensive testing
- Data privacy and model security concerns
- Transparency and fairness of AI decisions
- Need for human validation and oversight

AI must be deployed **responsibly and ethically**, especially in production
environments.
